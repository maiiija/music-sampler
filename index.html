BRIAN ENO

Photo of Brian Eno in 1974 from Wikimedia Commons.

Brian Eno (b. 1948) started his career as an art rock musician, similar to David Bowie and other contemporaries, with a focus on electronic instruments. His first solo album, Here Come the Warm Jets, was released in 1974 and primarily featured this sound. 1975's Another Green World, one of his most popular albums, featured a mix of art rock and ambient music, marking his transition into the genre he has been most known for. His most well-known ambient work, and most well-known work in general, was Ambient 1: Music for Airports. He is said to have created the ambient genre. Another of his more popular ambient works is Apollo: Atmospheres and Soundtracks. This album was made as a soundtrack for the documentary film For All Mankind, which was not released until 1989. "An Ending (Ascent)," the most popular track from the album, has been used in a variety of other films and TV shows.
